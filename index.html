My Website CSE-4550

Description
Small size companies including startups often utilize rapid team communication strategies (such as extreme programming, prototyping, and pair programming) to develop a software or complete a project at a rapid pace. For that purpose, teams utilize ticket management system (others call it Scrum stories) which are produced to assign tasks to different team members as well as their progress. The tickets are assigned based on the person’s expertise and their schedule on short-term (1-2 days, 1 week) to long-term based Sprint (1+ month). Tickets get distributed based on employee’s schedule and expertise which often also allow diverse skill development for employees via team collaboration. Tasks then get moved between different sections (from Ice box, design, implementation, testing, to a shippable product or feature) based on the reported progress and daily Scrum progress meetings. Implement a web-based ticket management system that consists of team projects (and subsystems of the projects divided into tasks). Tasks can be categorized into different categories (sprints, day, and weeklong tasks). For each task, an appropriate employee is selected given their schedule, skills, and their possible collaborative pair and their availability). Two major items are the employees and projects among which the scheduling is performed.
